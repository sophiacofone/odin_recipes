Doenjang Jjigae

Description

I came across this recipe when I was looking for more uses for the Doenjang and Gochujang I had bought for my mapo tofu. I learned that many Koreans eat Doenjang Jjigae as a comfort food! I tried it out, and have since ate it SO many times. It has a wonderful flavor and smell, and is a great way to eat tons of veggies!

The majority of this recipe comes from Maangchi. I have customized it in order to use ingredients that I have on hand. Serves 2-4.

Ingredients

  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 small zucchini, cut into ½-inch pieces (about 1 cup)
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and cut into ½-inch cubes (about 1 cup)
  • ½ cup shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 piece kombu
  • 1 cup katsuobushi
  • 4 cups water
  • 3 Tbsp Doenjang
  • ½ tsp Gochujang
  • 6 ounces medium-firm tofu, cut into ½-inch cubes (about 1 cup)
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Steps

  1. Add the water and kombu to a medium saucepan (or earthenware pot). If you have time, soak the kombu in water for 30 minutes. Do not remove white stuff on kombu.
  2. SLOWLY bring it to a boil on medium-low heat so you can extract as much umami from the kombu as possible (about 10 minutes). Right before the stock boils, remove the kombu and set it aside.
  3. Add the katsuobushi to the kombu dashi and let it simmer for 1 minute.
  4. Turn off the heat and strain through a fine-mesh sieve.
  5. Add the dashi to the saucepan and bring back to simmer.
  6. Add potato, onion, zucchini, mushrooms, and garlic. Cook for 15 minutes until potatos are done.
  7. Stir in the soybean paste, mixing well.
  8. Add the tofu and cook for another 3 minutes.
  9. Sprinkle with the green onions and serve as a side dish to rice.
